Sweet & sour Pork

Description

Sweet and sour pork (咕噜肉) sits among some of the most well-known Chinese dishes outside of China. It’s widely believed to be a Cantonese dish, known colloquially as gú lóuh yuhk, which roughly translates to “ancient pork.

Some tales say the name comes from the “guh guh guh” sound people make as they gulp down the delicious bites of pork.

Ingredients

  • Green Bell Pepper
  • Red Bell Pepper
  • Onion
  • Pineapple
  • Garlic
  • Pork shoulder roast
  • Boiling water
  • Potato flour
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Sesame oil
  • Lemon Juice
  • Sugar
  • Soy Sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Creamed Potato Flour

Instructions

  1. Marinade pork.
  2. Rack & warm oven.
  3. Sauce: Place all Sauce ingredients EXCEPT water in a large jug or small bowl. Mix until combined, then mix in water.
  4. Be Stir Fry Ready: Have all ingredients lined up, ready to toss in. After Fry #2, things move quickly.
  5. Heat oil and fry chicken.
  6. Heat oil in a very large skillet over high heat.
  7. Add garlic, ginger and onion, stir for 1 1/2 minutes. Add capsicum and stir for 2 minutes.
  8. Add Sauce & pinapple, then let it come to a rapid simmer. Simmer for 2 minutes until it thickens - when you drag the spoon across the base, a path should briefly appear (see video at 1 min 53 s).
  9. 10 second toss: Add pork, then QUICKLY toss to coat - aim for 10 seconds!
  10. Serve ASAP!
