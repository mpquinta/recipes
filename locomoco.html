Loco Moco

Description

Loco Moco is a classic and very popular Hawaiian comfort food made from a bed of rice topped with a ground beef patty and a fried egg smothered with rich gravy sauce. After doing a bit of research I have learnt that Loco Moco has quite an amusing origin. It is said that Loco Moco was created at the Lincoln Grill restaurant in Hilo, Hawaii by a request from a hungry group of Japanese-Hawaiian teenagers who wanted something cheap but filling. The teenagers decided to name the dish “loco moco” after their friend who was nicknamed ‘Crazy’ using the Spanish word for the name to create a catchy rhyme.

Nowadays this dish is famous worldwide and is especially popular in Japan as a western style (yoshoku) meal especially amongst children. There are many variations of loco moco but this version is a Japanese take on the dish using Japanese rice and hambagu style patties. It’s perfect for a weeknight dinner or an easy lunch on-the-go!

Ingredients

  • Cooked white rice
  • Burger patty
  • Eggs
  • Green onions
  • Beef broth
  • Onion
  • Mushrooms
  • Butter
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Ketchup
  • Flour
  • Cornstarch

Instructions

  1. Cook the rice.
  2. Prepare the patty and cook it.
  3. Put butter over medium heat in the same frying pan and saute the onions.
  4. Add the mushrooms to saute with the onion. When the mushrooms soften, add flour and stir.
  5. Pour beef stock, worcestershire sauce and ketchup.
  6. Keep stirring and turn the heat off when the mixture has thickened. Set it aside.
  7. Make sunny side eggs in a separate frying pan.
  8. Serve cooked rice in a bowl topped with the burger patty, the sunny side egg, then smother with mushroom and onion gravy sauce.
